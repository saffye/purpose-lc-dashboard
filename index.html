▶ PRIORITY FORMULA
FINAL SCORE
score = AttendanceScore × ConsumeMod × GearMod × LootMod × HardSoftPrio × BisMod
All scores are normalised so the top eligible player = 100. Modifiers are applied in sequence, each one multiplying the running total.
ATTENDANCE SCORE (primary driver)
tenure = 4 × log₂(raidsAttended + 1)
effPct = rawPct + (100 − rawPct) × (1 − 1/(1 + raids/8))
AttendanceScore = floor(effPct / 10) × tenure
Tenure uses a logarithmic curve — early raids matter most. Attending 2 vs 5 raids is a much bigger difference than 20 vs 25. The curve softens significantly after ~10 raids and flattens by ~20.

Effective attendance percentage forgives occasional absences for established raiders. A two-week vacation barely affects a 6-month veteran, but a new raider missing raids early is a red flag and is not forgiven. Raiders must have attended at least 2 raids to receive any score.
CONSUME MODIFIER (×0.5 – ×1.0)
ConsumeMod = 1 / (1 + missingPct / 100)
Per-raid scores are adjusted before averaging. Weapon oil is recomputed for Tanks and Physical DPS — if 2 or more players share the same weapon enhancement percentage on a given raid, it is treated as a raid buff coverage issue (Windfury, Paladin blessing) and forgiven. If a player is the only one at that value, it counts against them.

Additionally, outlier-low individual raids are partially forgiven based on track record. A player who is consistently well-consumed but had one bad raid will have that raid blended toward their personal median. Players with fewer than ~4 raids receive minimal forgiveness as there is insufficient track record.
GEAR MODIFIER (×0.5 – ×1.0)
GearMod = 1 / (1 + 0.1 × gearIssues)
Missing gems and enchants are always gear issues regardless of item quality. Suboptimal enchants (wrong or cheap enhancements) are additionally flagged if the item is ranked top 5 BiS for that player's spec — since top-5 items are unlikely to be replaced, using a budget enchant on them is not forgivable. Suboptimal enchants on non-BiS gear are ignored since the player is expected to replace the item soon. The penalty scales linearly — 5 issues is ×0.67, 10 issues is ×0.5.
LOOT SCORE MODIFIER (×0.3 – ×0.7)
LootMod = 1.0 − percentile × 0.667
Loot Score = (total item value received ÷ raids attended) × 100. Each item is assigned a numerical value reflecting its desirability, and a player's total received value is divided by their raids attended to normalise for tenure — a long-attending raider who received little is not unfairly penalised vs a newer player.

The modifier is a continuous curve within each role group — the player at the 0th percentile (received the least relative to peers) gets ×1.0 (no penalty), the player at the 100th percentile (received the most) gets ×0.33. This is smoother than a bucketed tier system and avoids cliff edges.
HARD & SOFT PRIORITY
Hard Prio: ×1.75  |  Soft Prio: ×1.25  |  Both: ×2.1875
Set by the loot council to reflect class/spec needs for specific items. Hard prio is a strong signal that the item is designed for that spec. Soft prio indicates preference. These multiply together if both apply. They are impactful but do not completely override a raider who has been waiting a very long time.
BIS UPGRADE MODIFIER (×0.5 – ×1.4)
>+20% EPV: ×1.4  |  +5–20%: ×1.2  |  ±5%: ×1.0  |  −5–20%: ×0.8  |  >−20%: ×0.5
Not on spec BiS list (others are): ×0.5  |  Item not on any BiS list: no modifier
EPV (Equivalence Point Value) measures an item's stat value relative to BiS for that spec. Upgrade percentage is computed relative to the player's current item in that slot. Thresholds are percentage-based rather than flat EPV so they scale correctly across slots — a 5% upgrade on a weapon and a 5% upgrade on a ring represent equally meaningful improvements relative to their slot.
