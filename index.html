▶ PRIORITY FORMULA
FINAL SCORE
score = AttendanceScore × ConsumeMod × GearMod × LootMod × HardSoftPrio × BisMod × AltMod
All scores are normalised so the highest-scoring eligible player = 100. Each modifier multiplies the running total — no single factor is additive.
ATTENDANCE SCORE (primary driver)
tenure = 4 × log₂(raidsAttended + 1)
effPct = rawPct + (100 − rawPct) × (1 − 1/(1 + raids/8))
AttendanceScore = floor(effPct / 10) × tenureAttendanceScore = floor(rawPct / 10) × tenure
Tenure follows a logarithmic curve, giving the most weight to early raids. The difference between attending 2 and 5 raids is much more significant than between 20 and 25. The curve flattens substantially after ~10 raids, preventing long-tenured veterans from being insurmountably ahead of established raiders.

The effective attendance percentage blends a veteran's raw percentage toward 100% based on tenure, so an occasional absence does not permanently harm a long-standing raider's priority. New raiders receive little or no forgiveness — consistent attendance in the early weeks is expected and indicative of long-term reliability.Raw attendance percentage is used with no veteran forgiveness — every absence counts equally regardless of raid history. Raiders must have attended at least 2 raids to receive any score.
CONSUME MODIFIER (×0.5 – ×1.0)
ConsumeMod = 1 / (1 + missingPct / 100)
forgiveness = 1 − 1 / (1 + raidsAttended / 8)
adjRaidScore = raidScore + (median − raidScore) × forgiveness  [if gap > 20]
Per-raid consume scores are adjusted before being averaged. For Tanks and Physical DPS, weapon enhancement percentage is recomputed per raid: if two or more players in those roles share the same value, it is treated as a raid-level coverage gap (e.g. missing Windfury) and forgiven.

Outlier-low individual raids are also partially forgiven based on track record. A consistently well-prepared player who had one poor raid will have that raid blended toward their personal median. Raiders with fewer than approximately four raids receive minimal forgiveness, as there is insufficient history to distinguish a pattern from an anomaly.
GEAR MODIFIER (×0.5 – ×1.0)
GearMod = 1 / (1 + 0.1 × gearIssues)
Missing gems and missing enchants are always counted as gear issues, regardless of item quality. Suboptimal enchants — correct slot but wrong or budget enhancement — are additionally flagged if the item is ranked top 10 BiS for that player's spec across any phase. Items of that calibre are unlikely to be replaced, so a suboptimal enchant is treated the same as a missing one. On non-top-10 items, suboptimal enchants are not penalised, as the player may reasonably be saving for an upgrade. The penalty scales linearly: 5 issues is ×0.67, 10 issues is ×0.5.
LOOT SCORE MODIFIER (×0.33 – ×1.0)
LootMod = 1.0 − percentile × 0.667
Each player's loot score reflects the weighted value of items they've received relative to their raids attended. Players are ranked within their role group — the highest scorer receives ×0.33, the lowest receives ×1.0. Hover any item in the loot received dropdown for a full breakdown of how it was weighted.
ITEM PRIORITIZATION
Multipliers stack: ×1.75 × ×1.25 = ×2.1875
Configured per item by the Loot Council. Prioritizations can target roles, classes and individuals.
SET BONUS PRIORITIZATION
Only applies to tier tokens  |  Only when player is exactly one piece away from 2pc or 4pc
Configured per tier set by the Loot Council. Piece count is checked against loot distribution and currently equipped gear.
ALTERNATIVES MODIFIER (×0.71 – ×1.0)
AltMod = 1 / (1 + alts × 0.05)
0 alts → ×1.0  |  1 alt → ×0.95  |  6 alts → ×0.77  |  ~8 alts → ×0.71 (soft cap ~30%)
Counts unique items on the player's BiS list in the same slot with EPV more than 5% above their currently equipped item, limited to the phase in which the considered item first appears.
BIS UPGRADE MODIFIER (×0.1 – ×2.0)
Within ±5%: ×1.0 (sidegrade)  |  Outside ±5%: ×(1.0 + pct×0.02), upgrades capped at +50%
Examples: +20%→×1.4  |  +50%→×2.0  |  −20%→×0.6  |  −50%→×0.1 (floor)
Not on this player's BiS list (but on others'): ×0.5  |  Not on any BiS list: no modifier applied
EPV (Equivalence Point Value) quantifies an item's stat contribution relative to the BiS ceiling for that spec. The upgrade percentage is computed against the player's current equipped item in the relevant slot, using the best EPV seen across all phases. Items within ±5% EPV of the current item are treated as sidegrades and receive no modifier. Beyond that threshold the modifier scales continuously at twice the upgrade percentage — a +20% upgrade gives ×1.4, a +50% upgrade gives ×2.0 (the cap). Downgrades scale symmetrically with a floor of ×0.1 at -50% or worse. Using percentage rather than flat EPV ensures the modifier scales correctly across slot types — a 10% upgrade on a weapon and a 10% upgrade on a ring carry equal relative weight.
ROLL RECOMMENDATION
Open Roll — no active non-receiver has a >5% EPV upgrade
Restricted — at least one active non-receiver has a >5% EPV upgrade
  → Sort all eligible non-receivers by priority score
  → Exclude anyone below priority 50  |  1 eligible: award outright  |  2 eligible: roll if gap <20%
  → Find the gap ≥10% that is most disproportionate to the gap before it (top 4)
  → Trim roll group until spread between top and bottom is ≤30%  |  Cap at 4 players
Open Roll when no one has a meaningful EPV upgrade; otherwise restricted. The cut point is the gap ≥10% that is most disproportionate relative to the gap before it — e.g. 100, 89, 74, 63 cuts after 89 because 89→74 (17%) jumps relative to 100→89 (11%), while 74→63 (15%) does not. EPV determines open vs restricted only, not who participates in the roll.
